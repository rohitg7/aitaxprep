## Key Fields for Tax Return

| 1098-T Box | Flows To | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Box 1 | Form 8863, Part III (education credits) | Qualified tuition and fees paid |
| Box 4 | May reduce prior year credit — see recapture rules | Adjustments to prior year amounts |
| Box 5 | Reduces qualified expenses for credit calculation | Scholarships and grants received |
| Box 6 | May increase current year qualified expenses | Adjustments to prior year scholarships |

## Calculating Qualified Education Expenses

```
Qualified Expenses = Box 1 - Box 5 + Box 6 - Box 4 - Box 10
```

If the result is negative (scholarships exceed tuition), the excess may be taxable income.

## Education Credits Decision Tree

```
Is this an eligible educational institution?
├── No → No credit available
└── Yes → Is the student in the first 4 years of post-secondary education?
    ├── Yes → Has the American Opportunity Credit been claimed for 4 tax years?
    │   ├── No → Consider American Opportunity Credit (Form 8863)
    │   │   ├── Up to $2,500 per student
    │   │   ├── 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000
    │   │   ├── 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
    │   │   └── Income limits apply — VERIFY against IRS
    │   └── Yes → Consider Lifetime Learning Credit
    └── No → Consider Lifetime Learning Credit (Form 8863)
        ├── Up to $2,000 per return (not per student)
        ├── 20% of first $10,000 of qualified expenses
        ├── NOT refundable
        └── Income limits apply — VERIFY against IRS
```

> ⚠️ **VERIFY** credit amounts and income phaseout limits against [Publication 970](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p970) and [Form 8863 instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8863).

## Triggers

- 1098-T received → Consider **Form 8863** (Education Credits)
- Qualified expenses > 0 → Evaluate American Opportunity Credit vs. Lifetime Learning Credit
- Box 5 > Box 1 → Excess scholarships may be taxable → **Schedule 1, Line 8r**
- Box 7 checked → Expenses may be for next year's academic period
- Box 8 checked → Student qualifies for American Opportunity Credit (half-time requirement)

## Important Notes

- **Cannot claim both** American Opportunity Credit and Lifetime Learning Credit for the same student in the same year
- The American Opportunity Credit is more valuable in most cases (higher maximum + partially refundable)
- Room, board, insurance, and transportation are NOT qualified expenses for education credits
- Books and supplies ARE qualified for American Opportunity Credit but NOT for Lifetime Learning Credit
- If someone else (e.g., parent) claims the student as a dependent, only the person claiming the dependent can take the credit
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 970](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p970) — Tax Benefits for Education
