## Calculating Taxable Social Security Benefits

The taxable portion of Social Security benefits depends on the taxpayer's **combined income** (also called provisional income).

### Step 1: Calculate Combined Income

```
Combined Income = AGI (without Social Security)
                + Tax-exempt interest
                + 50% of Net Social Security benefits (Box 5)
```

### Step 2: Determine Taxable Percentage

**Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse:**

| Combined Income | Taxable Portion of Benefits |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| Below $25,000 | 0% — benefits are not taxable |
| $25,000 – $34,000 | Up to 50% of benefits may be taxable |
| Above $34,000 | Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable |

**Married Filing Jointly:**

| Combined Income | Taxable Portion of Benefits |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| Below $32,000 | 0% — benefits are not taxable |
| $32,000 – $44,000 | Up to 50% of benefits may be taxable |
| Above $44,000 | Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable |

**Married Filing Separately (lived with spouse):**

| Combined Income | Taxable Portion of Benefits |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| Any amount > 0 | Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable |

> ⚠️ **VERIFY** these thresholds against [Publication 915](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p915) for the current tax year. These thresholds are NOT indexed for inflation and have remained unchanged since 1994.

### Step 3: Use the Social Security Benefits Worksheet

Use the **Social Security Benefits Worksheet** in the Form 1040 instructions (or Publication 915) to calculate the exact taxable amount for Line 6b.

## Triggers

- Box 5 > 0 → Must determine if any portion is taxable using the worksheet
- Combined income exceeds threshold → Portion of benefits becomes taxable
- Box 6 > 0 → Include in federal withholding on Form 1040 Line 25b

## Important Notes

- **RRB-1099** (Railroad Retirement Board) is treated similarly but is a separate form
- If the taxpayer received Social Security AND repaid benefits in the same year, the net amount (Box 5) is used
- If Box 5 is negative (repaid more than received), see Publication 915 for special rules
- Lump-sum election: If Box 3 includes benefits for prior years, the taxpayer may be able to use a special calculation — see [Publication 915](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p915)
