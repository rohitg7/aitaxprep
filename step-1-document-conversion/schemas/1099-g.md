## State/Local Tax Refund Taxability (Box 2)

The state or local tax refund in Box 2 is only taxable if the taxpayer **itemized deductions** in the prior year AND received a tax benefit from the state/local tax deduction.

```
Did taxpayer itemize deductions in the prior year?
├── No (took standard deduction) → Box 2 is NOT taxable — do not report
└── Yes → Did the state/local tax deduction provide a tax benefit?
    ├── Yes → Box 2 is taxable → Schedule 1, Line 1
    │   └── Use the State and Local Income Tax Refund Worksheet
    │       (IRS 1040 Instructions) to calculate taxable portion
    └── No (limited by SALT cap and would have itemized anyway) → NOT taxable
```

## Triggers

- Box 1 > 0 → **Schedule 1** required (unemployment compensation)
- Box 2 > 0 + prior year itemized → **Schedule 1** required (taxable refund)
- Box 6 > 0 → **Schedule 1** required (taxable grants)
- Box 7 > 0 → **Schedule F** required (agriculture payments)

## Important Notes

- Unemployment compensation is **fully taxable** as ordinary income
- The taxpayer may have elected voluntary withholding on unemployment (Box 4)
- RTAA (Reemployment Trade Adjustment Assistance) payments in Box 5 are taxable
- If Box 8 is checked, the refund in Box 2 relates to a trade or business and should be reported on Schedule C or other business form
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 525](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p525) for general rules on taxability of government payments
