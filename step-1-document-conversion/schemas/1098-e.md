## Student Loan Interest Deduction Rules

- **Maximum deduction**: $2,500 per year (VERIFY against IRS)
- **Above-the-line deduction**: Claimed on Schedule 1 — taxpayer does NOT need to itemize
- **Income phaseout**: The deduction phases out at higher income levels (VERIFY current limits)
- **Filing status restriction**: Cannot claim if filing status is married filing separately

```
Is the taxpayer filing as Married Filing Separately?
├── Yes → NO deduction allowed
└── No → Is the MAGI within the income limits?
    ├── Below phaseout start → Full deduction (up to $2,500 or Box 1, whichever is less)
    ├── Within phaseout range → Partial deduction (calculate prorated amount)
    └── Above phaseout end → NO deduction allowed
```

> ⚠️ **VERIFY** the income phaseout thresholds against [Publication 970](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p970) for the current tax year. These amounts are adjusted annually for inflation.

## Qualifying Conditions

The deduction is available only if:
1. The loan was taken out **solely** to pay qualified higher education expenses
2. The expenses were paid for the taxpayer, spouse, or dependent
3. The student was enrolled at least half-time in a degree program
4. The taxpayer is **not** claimed as a dependent on someone else's return
5. Filing status is **not** married filing separately

## Multiple 1098-Es

- If the borrower receives multiple 1098-E forms from different lenders, **sum all Box 1 amounts**
- The total deduction is still capped at $2,500 regardless of the number of loans
- Enter the combined total on Schedule 1, Line 21

## Triggers

- Box 1 > 0 → **Schedule 1** required (student loan interest deduction, Line 21)
- Multiple 1098-Es → Sum Box 1 amounts, cap at $2,500

## Important Notes

- Interest paid on loans from family members or employer plans does NOT qualify
- The deduction is for **interest** only — principal payments are not deductible
- Voluntary payments of interest during deferment or forbearance DO qualify
- If Box 2 is checked, Box 1 may not include all qualifying interest — the borrower may have additional deductible amounts (loan origination fees)
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 970](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p970) — Tax Benefits for Education
