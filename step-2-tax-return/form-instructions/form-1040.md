> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: These instructions are AI-optimized summaries, not the official IRS instructions. You MUST cross-validate every threshold, rate, and limit against the [official Form 1040 instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1040) and [current year IRS inflation adjustments](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-provides-tax-inflation-adjustments) before producing a final tax return.

## When to Use This Form

All U.S. individual taxpayers file Form 1040. It is always required as the main tax return form.

## Line-by-Line Instructions

### Income Section

#### Line 1a: Wages, Salaries, Tips
- **Source**: Sum of all W-2 Box 1 amounts
- **Calculation**: Add all W-2 documents' `box_1` values
- **Example**: If W-2 #1 box_1 = 60000 and W-2 #2 box_1 = 25000 → Line 1a = 85000.00
- **Verify**: Total should match the sum shown on all W-2s

#### Line 1z: Total from lines 1a through 1h
- **Calculation**: Sum of lines 1a through 1h
- **Note**: Lines 1b-1h are for household employee wages, tip income, Medicaid waiver payments, etc. Most filers only have Line 1a.

#### Line 2a: Tax-Exempt Interest
- **Source**: Sum of all 1099-INT Box 8 + 1099-DIV Box 12
- **Note**: This is informational — it does NOT add to taxable income

#### Line 2b: Taxable Interest
- **Source**: Sum of all 1099-INT Box 1 amounts
- **Trigger**: If total exceeds $1,500 → Schedule B is required
- **IRS Verification**: Check [Publication 550](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p550) for what counts as taxable interest

#### Line 3a: Qualified Dividends
- **Source**: Sum of all 1099-DIV Box 1b amounts
- **Note**: Qualified dividends are taxed at preferential capital gains rates

#### Line 3b: Ordinary Dividends
- **Source**: Sum of all 1099-DIV Box 1a amounts
- **Trigger**: If total exceeds $1,500 → Schedule B is required
- **Note**: Line 3a is a subset of Line 3b (qualified ⊆ ordinary)

#### Line 4a/4b: IRA Distributions
- **Source**: 1099-R forms where Box 7 code indicates IRA distribution
- **4a**: Total distribution amount
- **4b**: Taxable amount (may differ if non-deductible contributions were made)
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 590-B](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p590b)

#### Line 5a/5b: Pensions and Annuities
- **Source**: 1099-R forms for pensions
- **5a**: Total pension/annuity received
- **5b**: Taxable amount
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 575](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p575)

#### Line 6a/6b: Social Security Benefits
- **Source**: SSA-1099 Box 5 (net benefits)
- **6a**: Total social security benefits
- **6b**: Taxable amount (depends on total income — use worksheet in instructions)
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 915](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p915)

#### Line 7: Capital Gain or (Loss)
- **Source**: Schedule D, Line 21 (or Line 16 if no 28% rate gain or unrecaptured 1250 gain)
- **Prerequisite**: Must complete Form 8949 and Schedule D first
- **Cap on losses**: Net capital losses limited to -3000.00 (-1500.00 if married filing separately)
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 550](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p550) and [Schedule D instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1040sd)

#### Line 8: Other Income from Schedule 1
- **Source**: Schedule 1, Line 10
- **Includes**: Business income, alimony received, unemployment, gambling, etc.

#### Line 9: Total Income
- **Calculation**: Sum of lines 1z, 2b, 3b, 4b, 5b, 6b, 7, 8

#### Line 10: Adjustments to Income
- **Source**: Schedule 1, Line 26
- **Common adjustments**: Educator expenses, HSA deduction, IRA deduction, student loan interest, 50% SE tax deduction

#### Line 11: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
- **Calculation**: Line 9 - Line 10
- **Important**: AGI determines eligibility for many credits and deductions

### Deductions Section

#### Line 12: Standard Deduction or Itemized Deductions
- **Decision**: Use whichever is LARGER (standard vs. Schedule A total)
- **Standard deduction amounts** (VERIFY against current year IRS Rev. Proc.):
  - Single: Check IRS
  - Married Filing Jointly: Check IRS
  - Married Filing Separately: Check IRS
  - Head of Household: Check IRS
  - Add additional amount if age 65+ or blind
- **Exception**: If MFS and spouse itemizes → you MUST itemize
- **IRS Verification**: [current year standard deduction amounts](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-provides-tax-inflation-adjustments)

#### Line 13: Qualified Business Income Deduction
- **Applies if**: Self-employment income via Schedule C or K-1 pass-through income
- **Calculation**: Generally 20% of qualified business income (complex rules apply)
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 535](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p535) and [Form 8995 instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8995)

#### Line 14: Total Deductions
- **Calculation**: Line 12 + Line 13

#### Line 15: Taxable Income
- **Calculation**: Line 11 - Line 14
- **Minimum**: 0.00 (cannot be negative)

### Tax Computation Section

#### Line 16: Tax
- **Source**: Use tax table (if taxable income < $100,000) or Tax Computation Worksheet
- **Qualified dividends/capital gains**: If Line 3a > 0 or Line 7 > 0, use the Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet (lower rate)
- **IRS Verification**: Fetch current year tax brackets from [IRS Revenue Procedure](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-provides-tax-inflation-adjustments)
- **CRITICAL**: Do NOT use hard-coded tax rates. Verify against the current year's official rates.

#### Lines 17-24: Credits and Additional Taxes
- **Line 19**: Child tax credit — see Schedule 8812
- **Line 22**: Line 18 - Line 21 (tax after credits)
- **Line 23**: Other taxes from Schedule 2 (SE tax, etc.)
- **Line 24**: Total tax = Line 22 + Line 23

### Payments Section

#### Line 25a: Federal Tax Withheld from W-2s
- **Source**: Sum of all W-2 Box 2 amounts

#### Line 25b: Federal Tax Withheld from 1099s
- **Source**: Sum of all 1099 forms' federal withholding boxes (Box 4 on most 1099s)

#### Line 25d: Total Federal Tax Withheld
- **Calculation**: Line 25a + 25b + 25c

#### Line 33: Total Payments
- **Calculation**: Sum of lines 25d through 32

### Refund or Amount Owed

#### Line 34: Overpaid
- **Calculation**: Line 33 - Line 24 (only if positive)
- **If positive**: You're getting a refund

#### Line 35a: Refunded to You
- **Usually**: Same as Line 34 (unless splitting refund)

#### Lines 35b-35d: Direct Deposit
- **35b**: Bank routing number (9 digits)
- **35c**: Account type (checking or savings)
- **35d**: Account number

#### Line 37: Amount You Owe
- **Calculation**: Line 24 - Line 33 (only if positive)
- **If positive**: You owe additional tax

## Decision Trees

### Which Filing Status?
```
Are you married as of Dec 31?
├── Yes → Do you want to file together?
│   ├── Yes → married_filing_jointly
│   └── No → married_filing_separately
│       (Note: MFJ almost always gives lower tax)
└── No → Do you have a qualifying dependent?
    ├── Yes → Did you pay >50% of household costs?
    │   ├── Yes → head_of_household
    │   └── No → single
    └── No → Is your spouse deceased within last 2 years
            AND do you have a dependent child?
        ├── Yes → qualifying_surviving_spouse
        └── No → single
```

### Standard vs. Itemized Deduction
```
1. Look up standard deduction for filing status (VERIFY current year)
2. Calculate Schedule A total:
   - Medical expenses > 7.5% of AGI
   - State/local taxes (capped at $10,000)
   - Mortgage interest
   - Charitable contributions
3. If Schedule A total > standard deduction → Itemize
4. Exception: If MFS and spouse itemizes → Must itemize
```

### Which Schedules Are Needed?
```
Schedule 1: Any of these? → business income, capital gains not on D,
            alimony, unemployment, HSA deduction, SE tax deduction,
            IRA deduction, student loan interest, educator expenses
Schedule 2: AMT applies? SE tax? Net investment income tax?
Schedule 3: Foreign tax credit? Education credits? Other credits?
Schedule A: Itemizing deductions?
Schedule B: Interest > $1,500 OR dividends > $1,500?
Schedule C: Self-employment income?
Schedule D: Capital gains/losses?
Schedule E: Rental, royalty, partnership, S-corp income?
Schedule SE: Self-employment net profit > $400?
Form 8949: Any sales of stocks, bonds, crypto?
```
