> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: These instructions are AI-optimized summaries, not the official IRS instructions. You MUST cross-validate every threshold, rate, and limit against the [official Form 8949 instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8949) before producing a final tax return.

## When to Use This Form

Use Form 8949 to report:
- Sales or exchanges of stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs
- Sales of cryptocurrency or other digital assets
- Sales of other capital assets (collectibles, real estate for investment, etc.)
- Transactions reported on Form 1099-B

Form 8949 feeds into **Schedule D**. Complete Form 8949 first, then transfer totals to Schedule D.

## Form Structure

Form 8949 has two parts:
- **Part I**: Short-term transactions (assets held **1 year or less**)
- **Part II**: Long-term transactions (assets held **more than 1 year**)

Each part has three checkbox categories:

### Box Categories

| Box | Basis Reported to IRS? | 1099-B Received? | When to Use |
|-----|----------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **A** (short-term) / **D** (long-term) | Yes | Yes | Basis was reported to IRS — most common for stocks bought after 2011 |
| **B** (short-term) / **E** (long-term) | No | Yes | 1099-B received but basis NOT reported to IRS |
| **C** (short-term) / **F** (long-term) | N/A | No | No 1099-B received (private sales, crypto on some exchanges, etc.) |

**Separate Form 8949 for each box checked.** If you have transactions in Box A and Box B, you need two Part I sections (or two separate Form 8949s).

## Column-by-Column Instructions

Each transaction row has columns (a) through (h):

#### Column (a): Description of Property
- **Enter**: Security name, number of shares, or description
- **Example**: "100 sh AAPL", "0.5 BTC", "Rental property at 123 Main St"

#### Column (b): Date Acquired
- **Format**: MM/DD/YYYY
- **Source**: 1099-B or purchase records
- **If various**: Enter "VARIOUS" if acquired on multiple dates (e.g., mutual fund shares)

#### Column (c): Date Sold or Disposed Of
- **Format**: MM/DD/YYYY
- **Source**: 1099-B

#### Column (d): Proceeds (Sales Price)
- **Source**: 1099-B proceeds column
- **Enter**: Gross proceeds before commissions (in most cases, the 1099-B reports net proceeds)
- **Adjustment**: If 1099-B reports gross and you need to deduct commissions, include commission in column (g) adjustment

#### Column (e): Cost or Other Basis
- **Source**: 1099-B cost basis column (if reported) or your own records
- **Important**: If basis was NOT reported to the IRS (Box B/E), you MUST enter the correct basis
- **Includes**: Purchase price + commissions + fees

#### Column (f): Adjustment Code(s)
- **Enter**: One or more adjustment codes (see table below)
- **Leave blank**: If no adjustments are needed

#### Column (g): Amount of Adjustment
- **Enter**: The dollar amount of the adjustment
- **Positive**: Increases gain or decreases loss
- **Negative**: Decreases gain or increases loss

#### Column (h): Gain or (Loss)
- **Calculation**: Column (d) - Column (e) + Column (g)
- **Negative values**: Show in parentheses

## Adjustment Codes

| Code | Meaning | Effect on Column (g) |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| **W** | Wash sale — loss disallowed | Positive (add back the disallowed loss) |
| **B** | Basis reported to IRS is incorrect | Enter the correction amount |
| **T** | Short-term gain or loss — holding period more than 1 year | Reclassify to long-term |
| **O** | Other — see instructions | Various |
| **S** | Gain or loss on sale of main home | Enter exclusion as negative (reduces gain) |
| **H** | Gain or loss from installment sale | Related to Form 6252 |
| **D** | Market discount | Add accrued market discount |
| **E** | Expense of sale not on 1099-B | Negative (reduces gain) |
| **L** | Nondeductible loss — related party, wash sale other than code W | Positive |
| **M** | Multiple codes apply | See attached statement |
| **N** | Basis not reported to IRS but no adjustment needed | 0 |
| **Q** | Gift or inheritance — basis step-up/carryover | Enter basis correction |
| **X** | Tax-exempt bond premium amortization | Adjustment amount |

## Wash Sale Rules

A **wash sale** occurs when you sell a security at a loss and buy a **substantially identical** security within 30 days before or after the sale.

```
Did you sell a security at a loss?
├── No → No wash sale issue
└── Yes → Did you buy substantially identical securities within
          30 days before OR 30 days after the sale?
    ├── No → Loss is fully deductible — no adjustment needed
    └── Yes → WASH SALE
        ├── The loss is DISALLOWED (cannot deduct it)
        ├── The disallowed loss is ADDED to the basis of the replacement shares
        ├── On Form 8949: Code "W" in column (f), disallowed amount in column (g)
        └── The holding period of the replacement shares includes the original holding period
```

- **1099-B reporting**: Most brokers report wash sales and show the disallowed amount
- If the 1099-B already reflects the wash sale adjustment, you may not need additional adjustments on Form 8949
- **VERIFY**: Compare your 1099-B wash sale amounts against your own records

## Processing 1099-B Transactions

### When No Adjustments Are Needed (Most Common)

If the 1099-B reports the correct basis and there are no adjustments:
- Check Box A (short-term) or Box D (long-term)
- You can enter totals directly on **Schedule D, Line 1a or Line 8a** instead of listing each transaction on Form 8949
- This shortcut only applies to Box A / Box D transactions with no adjustments

### When Adjustments ARE Needed

If any of the following apply, you must list each transaction on Form 8949:
- Wash sales
- Basis is incorrect on the 1099-B
- You need to report a different cost basis than shown
- You need to add expenses of sale

## Summary Totals

At the bottom of each Part (I and II), total columns (d), (e), (g), and (h):

| Column | Part I Totals Flow To | Part II Totals Flow To |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| (d) Total proceeds | Schedule D, Line 1b/2/3 col (d) | Schedule D, Line 8b/9/10 col (d) |
| (e) Total cost basis | Schedule D, Line 1b/2/3 col (e) | Schedule D, Line 8b/9/10 col (e) |
| (g) Total adjustments | Schedule D, Line 1b/2/3 col (g) | Schedule D, Line 8b/9/10 col (g) |
| (h) Total gain/(loss) | Schedule D, Line 1b/2/3 col (h) | Schedule D, Line 8b/9/10 col (h) |

The specific Schedule D line depends on which box was checked:
- Box A → Schedule D, Line 1b
- Box B → Schedule D, Line 2
- Box C → Schedule D, Line 3
- Box D → Schedule D, Line 8b
- Box E → Schedule D, Line 9
- Box F → Schedule D, Line 10

## Decision Tree: Which Box?

```
Is the transaction short-term (held ≤ 1 year)?
├── Yes → Part I
│   ├── 1099-B received AND basis reported to IRS? → Box A
│   ├── 1099-B received AND basis NOT reported? → Box B
│   └── No 1099-B received? → Box C
└── No (long-term, held > 1 year) → Part II
    ├── 1099-B received AND basis reported to IRS? → Box D
    ├── 1099-B received AND basis NOT reported? → Box E
    └── No 1099-B received? → Box F
```

## Triggers

- Any 1099-B received → **Form 8949** + **Schedule D**
- Wash sales present → Adjustments required on Form 8949
- Basis not reported on 1099-B → Must provide correct basis on Form 8949
- Cryptocurrency sales → Form 8949 (and check digital assets question on Form 1040 page 1)

## Important Notes

- **Holding period**: Date acquired to date sold. If "various", you may need to break into separate lots
- **Specific identification**: If you sold specific lots, ensure the basis matches what you identified at the time of sale
- **Cryptocurrency**: Treat as property — each sale, exchange, or use is a taxable event
- **Inherited assets**: Generally receive a stepped-up basis to fair market value at date of death — use code "Q" if adjusting
- **Gifted assets**: Generally carry over the donor's basis — may need adjustment code
- **IRS Verification**: [Publication 550](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p550) — Investment Income and Expenses
